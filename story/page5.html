Tantalus' second punishment was an eternal one. He stood in a swampy lake where the water reached his chin, but would soak down into the mud whenever he tried to drink it. Above him was a tree drooping with the most delicious and ripest fruits, but whenever he tried to pull them down "a gust of wind would blow them away into the shadowing clouds". He could never satisfy his hunger or thirst, as it was always just out of his reach.
